# Dwarven Keeps
**Capital:** -

**Common Races:** Dwarves, Gnomes
**Uncommon Races:** Dueregar, Half-Orcs, Tieflings

*The dwarven keeps mark many old castles and fort built with only a small part visible to the outer world and far more buried in the mountains and caves. The Keeps are known for trading rare minerals, metals and smithing tools, weaponry and armor.*

*Many of the keeps nowadays are however abandoned or in a state of decline. There exist keeps not even inhabited anymore, often instead taken up by other creatures.*
## Terrain & Geography
The Keeps are almost always built into mountainsides, ravines or large caves expanding into large underground networks. Many of the keeps go down into the [[Underdark]] and some say that in days past dwarves even traveled through the [[Underdark]] between keeps.

It is generally dark and cold inside the keep meaning that it makes poor living for anyone who does not posses darkvision or other means to see in the dark.
## Economy
**Resources:** A variety of metals, Gems, Art, Tools, Weaponry, Armor, Inventions
**Technology:** Steam power, Notable use of gunpowder, Skilled metalworking
## Politics
Many of the dwarven keeps are ruled by a council or a prince who rules the area. There is however no unifying figure who holds power over all keeps and each keep is unique.

There is a general distrust towards outsiders, especially [[Greenskin]]s, elves and humans, that said the keeps often trade a great deal with close villages and cities for food and other supplies.
## Culture
Many of the keeps hold a strong culture of crafting whether it be weapons, art or alloys. One can become famous in the city based on the quality of ones work.

The keeps generally don't have aristocratic families in the same ways some kingdoms do. That said there is a strong clan culture among dwarves where many stand based on the work of their ancestors. Similarly it is considered a great social crime to disgrace your clan and ancestors.

Many do not venture from the keeps except for exploring the mines. Heading to the surface is generally seen with distrust or even disdain, most who leave the keeps to travel the surface have in some way been cast out. In this way it is generally seen as leaving your heritage when you leave a keep, unless you do so for a specific reason (generally politics, war, marriage, trade).

Inside the Keeps magic is not generally encouraged and there is a certain disdain towards it as one should aim to make honest work with their hands. This is however not present when forging magical artifacts which are seen in a totally different light.

## Relations
While each keep is unique the following is general rule (With exceptions):
- The keeps don't interfere in foreign wars and don't forge alliances or engage in diplomacy.
- That said the keep often trade with nearby cities and villages to a large extent trading away artisan goods for food. This usually means that the keeps have warm relations with atleast the nearby towns and cities.
- Even if the keeps are in another kingdom they rarely recognize that countries authority over them.
## History
**Ancient History:** Many of the Keeps have been built in times long forgotten (even by the long memories of dwarves and gnomes). That said the dwarven keeps are generally in decline seeing to their historical heights. This means that there exists many abandoned and forgotten keeps spread throughout the lands.

The reason for the abandonment are mostly:
- The [[War of the beards]] reduced the dwarven, dueregars and gnomish populations notably. This combined with their slow reproduction means the population is far from recovering.
- The increased centralisation of power have ment that there are an increasing number of kingdoms not comfortable with "independent" keeps in their lands.
- The  [[Greenskin]]s have ment that many of the more forward outposts have been abandoned due to the continuous raids.
