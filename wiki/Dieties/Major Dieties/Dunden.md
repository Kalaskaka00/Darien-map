# Dunden

**Other Names:** The divine messenger, the great smith

**Aspects:** Storms, Winds, Duels, Smithing, Honor

**Assosciated Races:** Aaracokra, Kenkus, Owlin

**Domains:** Forge, Grave, Tempest, War

**Symbol:** Two crossing lightning bolts, an avil, five feathers

**Description:** Dunden is often described as a messenger, wielding a golden spear, wearing light leather armor and carying a satchel of letters as well as some smithing tools. Dunden is often shown as an Aaracokra, Kenku or Owlin or non descript birdperson.

Dundeen is often thought of as honarable, wift and stoic. He has a duality of booth being a great messenger and honarable warrior while simoltaniously being a harbringer of doom and ill fortune.

Dunden is against dishonarable acts especially in battle. He generally sees porly on people who act against honor or unjustly against others, he similarly dislikes deciet. He is said to value people who put hard work into any sort of craft.

**Worship:** Dunden is often worshiped at an anvil, preferably placed so wind can sweep through it. Any such anvil is usually suronded by letters people want sent to their loved oens be they live or dead as well as failed artisan crafts to show the hard work that was laid down even if it failed.

Worship to Dunden is often performed by creating crafts in any way one can. Usually the value is not in the result but rather the time, effort and devotion put into the craft. It is customary to then break the craft atop an anvil devoted to Dunden and leaving iot beside the anvil.

Dunden burials are usually made as open air burials where the person is left to the elements at a secluded spot. It is here customary that each person who know them leave some sort of letter or item they want sent to them in the afterlife.
