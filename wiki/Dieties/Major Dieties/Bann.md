# Bann

**Other Names:** Father of beasts, Crooked Moon

**Aspects:** Monsters, War, The Moon, Tides, Trickery

**Associated Races:** Bugbears, Goblins, Orcs, Sea-Elves, Satyrs, Shadar-Kai

**Domains:** Light, Nature, Trickery, War

**Symbol:** A cickle moon, a sword layered over a full moon, a crooked shortsword

**Description:** Bann is often described as a warrior, wielding two crooked shortswords, wearing chainmail and caring a bag of sand in his belt. Bann is often desribed as an Orc.

Bann is often thought of as wrathfull, as a trickster and as powerfull. He has the duality of both bringing destruction while also representing rightfull wrath.

Bann is against showing weakness and showing compassion to ones enemies. He generally despises words like honor and oaths and only seem them as hindrances or something which might be used to trick people. Except for this he is known as the god of the night bringing both dangerous and beatiful beasts. 

**Worship:** Bann is normally worshiped in the open under the moonlight. Ocassionally monuments or altars are built often depicting moons and tides, carved atop those altars are often the names of fallen warriors.

People who want to dedicate themselves to Bann often bleed some drops of blood during a full moon, preferably into a stream/river or the ocean, but if that is not available bleeding into the ground is also acceptable. The weapon one inflicted the wound with is often said to be blessed by Bann after this.

Some of the more extreme factions of Bann will sacrifice living creatures by cutting their throat and letting them bleed out in the full moon.

Bann burials often consist of burning a creature in fire together with its weapon and armor, if it died fighting the weapons of the enemies are also often burned. A normal way to show respect at such burials are to drop some blood into the flames.
