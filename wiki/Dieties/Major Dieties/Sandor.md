# Sandor

**Other Names:** 

**Aspects:** Oceans, Rivers, Fish, Trading, Oaths

**Associated Races:** Humans, Locathah, Tortles, Triton

**Domains:** Forge, Life, Order, Tempest

**Symbol:** A coin with an oar on it, a great whale, a wagon wheel

**Description:** Sandor is often described as a sailor, wielding a trident, carrying a coin pouch and a book of recorded oaths. They are often shown as riding a great whale. Sandor is most of time described as a human before her Godhood and a Locathah after ascending.

Sandor is often thought of as calm, calculating and fair. She has a duality of being fair and calm while simeltaniously able to create horrific events and send horrible sea beasts at travelers.

Sandor is against stealing and killing. Sandor is known as a keeper of oath and is said to see oathbreaking as the greatest offence one can commit. Usually Sandor sees kindly on people helping fellow travelers.

**Worship:** Sandor is often worshiped by the rivers, lakes or ocean or atleast where the altar is in visual range of it. Sandors altars are often made of sandtstone, shells and other ocean and river treasures. They often have carvings of ships coins and fish around them.

A normal tradition amongst those traveling the seas or making their living of the seas is to pay tribute to Sandor. This is often done by throwing a coin or minor trasure into the ocean where ones payment is meant to match ones weath. Paying tribute is said to lessen the risk of Sandor wrath and may even give great currents to ones help.

Those who dedicate themselves to sandor usually are put into the ocean, rivers or lakes until their lungs are filled with water and are latter pulled out and are then pulled out recucitated. This is meant to bring the eyes of Sandor upon one, and is a test if one i detirmined as worthy by Sandor.

Snadoir burials are most often conducted by binding a weight to someone and throwing them down a lake, river or the sea. For captains the custom is building them a makeshift sea or raft and sending it out on the ocean.
