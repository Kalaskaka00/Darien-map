# Rendeen

**Other Names:** The Huntress, Dawn flower

**Aspects:** Hunting, Wildlife, Archery, Forests, Seasons

**Associated Races:** Centaurs, Eladrin, Fairies, Firbolgs, Harengons, Tabaxi, Wood Elves

**Domains:** Life, Nature, Trickery, Twilight

**Symbol:** A great oak, three corssed arrows, a stag head

**Description:** Rendeen is often described as a hunter, wielding a great bow, wearing a wolf pelt over their back and carrying a bag of seeds in their belt. Normally Rendeen is thought of as a she and described as a Wood Elf or Eladrin. 

Rendeen is often thought of as cunning, observative and patient. She has a duality for caring for nature and all creatures in it while simeltaniously being ruthless to those who would disturb the balance of nature.

Rendeen is normally not against killing animals, collecting plants and other things that can be done sustianably in harmony with the greens. Especially offensive to her are trophy hunting or hunting for "fun", chopping down old trees and burning or harming the woods. Generally she is against "arranging" nature for example by triming trees are carefully planting hedges in straight lines and so on.

**Worship:** Normally Rendeen is worshipped at a great tree (preferably oak) that is decorated at its base by different carved symbols, when trees are not available altars of wood are sometimes constructed.

A normal thing to dedicate oneself to Rendeen is to plant a tree and tend to it for atleast 1 year. Many clerics however keep comming to their tree until their death.

Rendeen burials normally consist of burying someone in the ground and planting a tree or other plant atop the grave.
## A story
There once was a hunter greater with a bow than anyone. He kept comming with more and more food to fed his tribe. He himself claimed to be blessed by Rendeen to feed his tribe.

He kept bringing more and more and bigger prey, until oen day he brought more food than the tribe could eat, forcing them to leave the food behind. The day after he came back empty handed, all his arrows had indeed flew falsley and it seemed like the woods themselves had concealed any pray from him.

He went back the day after more detirmined than ever and in the end found a great stag, he thought first that this stag was one step to far, it might even be a nature spirit or forest guardian. However, when he thought of the glory of felling a stag of that size he in the end shot towards it, his arrow however flew untrue. Enraged he charged the stag with his dagger and killed it.

He drew the large stag back to the tribe, the tribe where horrified over the killing of this stag and refused to eat it and insisted on leaving it in the forest. The young hunter however ate the stag, took the antlers as a trophy and burnt the rest.

It was however in the morning he awoke only to find he had indeed shrank, being shorter. He tried to call for help but found his word could form no words. He tried to reach for his priced antlers only to see they were gone and his hands were cloves. It was at that point he heard the sound of humans and saw an arrow flying towards him, forcing him to run.

Since that day it is said that the stag hunter is now a stag that is cursed to always be hunted.
